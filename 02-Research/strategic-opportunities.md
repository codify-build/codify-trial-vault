# Strategic Opportunities

Derived from [[gap-analysis]], [[soul]], [[offer]], [[audience]].

---

## Opportunity 1: Own the Category — "Knowledge Preservation" Not "Automation"

**The gap it closes:** Gap 2 (positioning), Gap 1 (pricing)

Nobody in mid-market manufacturing automation is talking about knowledge preservation. They're all talking about efficiency, throughput, labor replacement. We're the only ones saying: "The machine isn't the asset. What your people know about running it is."

That's a category claim. Category claims justify premium pricing, shorten sales cycles, and attract referrals because they're easy to describe to a peer.

**What this looks like in practice:**
- Rename the core offer: from "automation consulting" to something like "Decision Logic Capture" or "Knowledge Continuity System"
- Rewrite all positioning language to lead with knowledge preservation, not automation
- Lead every sales conversation with the Frank story — not a capabilities overview
- LinkedIn content and ads built around "the retirement problem nobody is solving"

**Priority:** High. This doesn't require new delivery mechanics — it's a reframe of what already exists.

## Opportunity 2: Raise Rates to Market Minimum — $250/Hour, $25K–$60K Projects

**The gap it closes:** Gap 1 (pricing)

We've acknowledged competitors charge $250/hour and deliver less. The only thing standing between us and $250/hour is not making the ask. There is no value gap. There is a confidence gap.

**What this looks like in practice:**
- Set new rate for all new engagements at $250/hour minimum
- Repackage implementation tiers: $25K entry, $40K standard, $60K full Decision Logic Capture + implementation
- Position the premium as: you're not paying for hours. You're paying for 32 years of failure mode pattern recognition that would cost you six months of relearning if it walked out the door.
- Grandfather no existing clients — this is a new positioning, not a renegotiation

**Projected impact:** On a 200-hour annual consulting run rate, $70/hour increase = $14,000 in recovered revenue before any new clients added. Implementation repricing from $40K to $60K average = 50% revenue increase per project.

**Priority:** High. Implement before the next new engagement is scoped.

## Opportunity 3: Build a Proactive Diagnostic — The "Frank Risk Assessment"

**The gap it closes:** Gap 4 (pipeline), Gap 5 (proof)

Right now clients come to us after the Frank has left. The smarter play: help them identify who their Frank is before that person retires.

A short diagnostic — call it a Knowledge Risk Assessment or a Frank Assessment — maps which employees hold uncaptured institutional knowledge and estimates what it would cost if they left tomorrow. It could be delivered as:
- A free lead magnet (PDF or short workshop)
- A paid 2-day on-site engagement ($3,500–$5,000) that feeds directly into a full Capture project
- A content hook for LinkedIn: "5 questions to find out if your operation is one retirement away from a six-month setback"

This solves the pipeline problem and builds the proof asset simultaneously. Every assessment completed is a documented case that can become a future testimonial.

**Priority:** Medium-high. Requires some packaging work but the methodology already exists — it's Phase 1 of the existing delivery.

## Opportunity 4: Apply the Framework Internally — Capture Our Own Decision Logic

**The gap it closes:** Gap 3 (scalability), Gap 5 (proof)

We have the same problem our clients have. Our 32-year failure mode library is in our heads. If we applied our own framework to ourselves — conducted an internal Decision Logic Capture — we'd solve the scalability problem and create the most credible proof asset in our portfolio.

"We built this framework because we lost our Frank. Then we used it on ourselves."

That story — living proof that the methodology works at the firm level — is worth more than any client testimonial because it shows we believe it enough to do it ourselves.

**Priority:** Medium. Strategic long-game move. Worth planning now, executing over next 90 days.

---

## Opportunity 5: Reframe the Sales Conversation Around Retirement Math

**The gap it closes:** Gap 4 (pipeline), Gap 2 (positioning)

Prospects are easier to reach before they're in pain than after. The entry conversation isn't "you need automation." It's: "Who in your operation retires in the next 3 years? What would it cost you if they left tomorrow without a knowledge transfer?"

Turn that into a simple calculation — fully-loaded cost of six months of re-learning, error rates during the gap, productivity loss — and you have a number. Then compare that number to the cost of a Capture engagement. The ROI closes itself.

Content play: LinkedIn post or short article — "How much did Frank cost you?" Walk through the six months of relearning math. No pitch. Just math.

**Priority:** High for content and sales conversation. Low effort, high leverage.
