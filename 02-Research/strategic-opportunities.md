# Strategic Opportunities

Three opportunities surfaced from the context extraction that the executive likely can't see because he's too close to his own expertise.

---

## Opportunity 1: The Knowledge Insurance Product

**What:** Package "institutional knowledge capture" as a standalone product — $25K-$50K engagement. Before automating anything, capture the decision logic of their top 3-5 people.

**Why he can't see it:** He thinks of knowledge capture as a byproduct of implementation, not a product. But manufacturers will pay to de-risk their business BEFORE automation — especially after losing a "Frank."

**Market evidence:** 42% of manufacturing institutional knowledge lives in individual heads (Deloitte, 2024). The manufacturing workforce is aging — 25% of skilled workers eligible for retirement within 5 years. Succession planning gaps represent a $31B problem across mid-market manufacturing alone.

**Pricing:** $25K-$50K per engagement vs current $15K-$40K implementation. Higher margin, zero implementation risk, faster delivery. This is pure expertise.

## Opportunity 2: The $275/hr Repositioning

**What:** Raise consulting rate from $180/hr to $275/hr immediately. Reposition from "manufacturing consultant" to "pattern recognition specialist" — not hourly labour, but access to 32 years of failure-mode expertise that prevents six-figure mistakes.

**Why he can't see it:** Classic expert underpricing. He compares to his own historical rate, not the market value of what he delivers. His competitors charge $250/hr and deliver less — he said so himself.

**Market evidence:** Specialised manufacturing consultants in the $2M-$15M segment command $225-$350/hr. Firms positioning on "institutional knowledge preservation" (a category he could own) charge premium rates because the alternative — losing a Frank — costs $500K+ in relearning time.

**Revenue impact:** At current volume, $180→$275 = 53% revenue increase from existing clients with zero additional work. That's the gap between "consultant" and "the person who's seen every failure mode."

**The positioning shift:** Stop saying "consulting." Start saying "32 years of pattern recognition, on demand."

## Opportunity 3: The Knowledge Capture Methodology — Licensed

**What:** License his knowledge capture process to other mid-market manufacturers as a methodology + tools package. Not consulting — a system. Recurring $497-$997/mo.

**Why he can't see it:** He sees himself as a consulting firm, not a knowledge architecture firm. But his real IP isn't the manufacturing expertise alone — it's the PROCESS of knowing what to capture, what to ignore, and how to structure it so it survives personnel changes.

**Why his expertise qualifies him:** 32 years of knowing which knowledge matters. He's seen every failure mode — he knows which tolerances are critical and which are noise. Nobody else can design this capture process because nobody else has the pattern recognition to know what's worth preserving.

**The play:** Document the "Frank Methodology." Package it. License it. Every manufacturer with a senior person approaching retirement is a buyer — and there are thousands of them.
