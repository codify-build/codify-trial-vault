# Gap Analysis

Derived from [[soul]], [[offer]], [[audience]], and [[proof]].

---

## Gap 1: Pricing — 28–38% Below Market Rate

**Current:** $180/hour consulting, $15K–$40K implementation
**Market:** Competitors charge $250/hour and deliver less by the client's own assessment

The gap isn't value — it's positioning. We've never repositioned. We're charging 2019 rates for 2026 expertise. Every hour billed at $180 is $70 of value left on the table. On a 10-hour engagement that's $700. On a 200-hour annual run rate that's $14,000 in recovered revenue from a single rate correction.

**Root cause:** We don't have a category claim that justifies premium pricing. "Manufacturing automation consultant" commoditizes us. "Institutional knowledge architect" doesn't exist yet — which is exactly why it can command a premium.

---

## Gap 2: Positioning — Selling Automation, Not the Real Product

**What we sell:** Automation consulting and implementation
**What we actually deliver:** Knowledge preservation before retirement erases it

Automation is a delivery mechanism. Knowledge continuity is the transformation. Every competitor talks about the system. Nobody is talking about the knowledge underneath it. We have a category claim sitting unclaimed.

Positioning as automation consultants puts us in a crowded, price-competitive market. Positioning as the firm that solves "the Frank problem" puts us in a category of one.

**Root cause:** We never named what we do differently. The insight is in [[soul]] — it just hasn't been translated into positioning language yet.

---

## Gap 3: Scalability — We Have the Problem We Solve

**The irony:** Our institutional knowledge — 32 years of failure mode pattern recognition, decision logic, supplier quirk libraries — is trapped in our heads. We can't scale the firm because we haven't applied our own framework to ourselves.

This is both a business risk and an unspoken credibility asset. We understand the problem so well we're living it.

**Root cause:** The cobbler's children have no shoes. We help clients capture decision logic. We haven't done it for ourselves.

---

## Gap 4: Pipeline — Clients Come When They're Already in Pain

**Current state:** Clients engage us after a Frank has already left. We're called in for damage control, not prevention.

**Opportunity:** The retirement wave in mid-market manufacturing is predictable. SHRM and BLS data consistently shows skilled manufacturing workers retiring at accelerating rates. Any plant manager can look at their org chart and identify who's within 3–5 years of retirement. That's the entry conversation — not "we lost someone" but "we can see who's leaving."

**Root cause:** No proactive lead generation tool or diagnostic that surfaces risk before it materializes.

---

## Gap 5: Proof Assets — The Frank Story Is the Only One

Right now proof is carried by:
- The Frank story (personal, powerful, but ours — not a client outcome)
- 32 years in manufacturing / $4M operation experience
- "Solve problems engineers can't" claim

Missing:
- Named client outcomes from implementation projects
- Before/after examples of what was in the SOP vs. what Phase 1 surfaced
- Failure modes caught during Capture phase that would have broken the automation

**Root cause:** We haven't systematically collected and formatted client proof. It exists — it's in the work — but it hasn't been written up.
