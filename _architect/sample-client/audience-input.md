## /extract audience — Answers

**Q1: "Describe the last person who bought from you. What were they struggling with?"**
> Dave Chen, CEO of Pacific Ridge Manufacturing. $22M revenue, 180 employees, makes precision components for aerospace. He was stuck at $22M for three years. Tried hiring a COO — didn't work. Tried implementing Lean — half-adopted, created more confusion. His margins were eroding because his quoting process was still based on spreadsheets his founder built in 2009. He was personally approving every quote over $50K, which meant he was the bottleneck for 60% of their revenue.

**Q2: "What did they try before they found you?"**
> Dave had been through the usual gauntlet. McKinsey did a "digital readiness assessment" — $180K for a PDF. Then he hired a fractional COO from one of those matching platforms — good guy, but he'd never run a manufacturing floor. Tried to apply SaaS scaling playbooks to a shop floor. Lasted 8 months. Then a buddy at his YPO chapter told him about me.

**Q3: "What words do THEY use to describe their problem?"**
> "I feel like I'm running on a treadmill." "We should be at $35M by now but I can't get out of my own way." "I'm the smartest person in every meeting and that's the problem." "My team is great at executing but nobody owns anything." The big one I hear constantly: "I built this thing and now it can't run without me."

**Q4: "What's the moment they decide they need help?"**
> It's usually after a failed hire or a lost contract. Dave's was losing a $3M contract because their quote took 11 days and the competitor did it in 3. That's when the pain becomes undeniable. It's never a strategic epiphany — it's always a specific, painful loss that makes them pick up the phone.

**Q5: "Who should NOT buy from you?"**
> Anyone under $10M revenue — they can't afford me and they don't have enough operational complexity to justify it. Founders who want a yes-man — I'm going to tell them things they don't want to hear. CEOs who think the problem is their people — it's almost never the people, it's the system. If you're looking for someone to blame your team, hire someone else. And anyone who wants a 200-page strategy deck — I don't write reports, I fix operations.
