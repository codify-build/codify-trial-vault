## /extract soul — Answers

**Q1: "If someone at a party asked what you do, what would you say?"**
> I help mid-market manufacturing CEOs stop bleeding money on operational inefficiency. Most of them are running $15-40M businesses on systems they built when they were a $3M shop. I rebuild their operational architecture so they can actually scale without hiring three more VPs they can't afford.

**Q2: "What do you believe about your industry that most people get wrong?"**
> Everyone thinks digital transformation is a technology problem. It's not. It's a leadership sequencing problem. I've watched companies spend $2M on ERP implementations that failed because the CEO didn't restructure decision-making authority first. You can't automate a broken chain of command.

**Q3: "Describe a moment when you knew your approach was different."**
> I was consulting for a plastics manufacturer in Ohio. They'd hired McKinsey before me — got a 200-page deck, beautiful charts. Nothing changed. I spent two weeks on their shop floor, talked to every shift supervisor, and found that the real bottleneck was a handoff between second and third shift that nobody in the C-suite even knew existed. Fixed it in 10 days. Saved them $1.2M annually. That's when I knew — the answers are always on the floor, never in the boardroom.

**Q4: "What would you never do, even if it made you more money?"**
> I'd never sell a retainer without a diagnostic first. Half the consultants in my space sell 12-month engagements on a 90-minute discovery call. That's malpractice. I need 2-3 weeks inside the operation before I'll even quote a scope. Some prospects hate that. Good — those aren't my clients.

**Q5: "If you retired tomorrow, what knowledge would walk out the door?"**
> How to read a manufacturing floor in 48 hours. I can walk into any mid-market plant and tell you within two days where the real problems are — not the ones management thinks they have, but the ones that are actually killing their margins. It's pattern recognition from 30 years of seeing the same failure modes across different industries. You can't Google that.

**Q6: "What process or method do you use that nobody else talks about?"**
> I call it the "Operational Gravity Map." Before I touch anything, I map every decision point in the operation and measure how long each decision takes to reach the person who actually executes it. Most companies have 6-8 decision layers. Best-in-class have 3. The gap between those numbers is where the money is hiding.

**Q7: "What do your best clients say about you that you'd never say about yourself?"**
> My best client — CEO of a $38M metal fabrication company — told his board I was "the most expensive consultant they'd ever hired and the only one who'd paid for himself in the first quarter." Another one said I was "allergic to bullshit." I'll take both.
