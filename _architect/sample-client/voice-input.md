# Sample Client: Marcus Webb — Voice Extraction Inputs

---

## /extract voice — Answers

**Q1: "Which sounds most like you? A, B, or C?"**
> C — "I've been in the trenches. Here's what actually matters." But honestly it's a mix of B and C. I walk people through what works, but I'm not academic about it. I've got the scars. When I say something works, it's because I've done it 50 times, not because I read a Harvard Business Review article about it.

**Q2: "What words or phrases do you say all the time in meetings?"**
> "Where's the decision bottleneck?" "Who actually owns this?" "Show me the floor, not the dashboard." "The spreadsheet is lying to you." "What does the shift supervisor say?" I also say "that's a $200K problem hiding in plain sight" a lot — because it usually is.

**Q3: "What marketing language makes you cringe?"**
> "Unlock your potential." "Synergy." "Digital transformation journey." "Thought leader." Anything with "leverage" used as a verb. "Scalable solutions" — what does that even mean? And the worst one: "We're like McKinsey but for mid-market." No. McKinsey is why they're calling me.

**Q4: "Paste an email or message you wrote recently that felt like 'you.'"**
> Here's one I sent to a prospect last week:
>
> "Tom — I looked at your P&L and your org chart. You've got 4 people between a customer order and the person who schedules production. That's not a staffing problem, that's an architecture problem. Happy to spend a day at your plant and show you where the drag is. If I can't find $500K in 3 weeks, I'll refund the diagnostic. Let me know."
>
> That's how I write. Short. Specific. No fluff.

**Q5: "How do you want people to feel after reading something from you?"**
> I want them to feel like someone finally said the thing they've been thinking but couldn't articulate. And then I want them to feel slightly uncomfortable — because if I'm doing my job, I'm pointing at something they've been avoiding. Not in a mean way. In a "you know this is true and now someone's said it out loud" way. Then I want them to feel like there's a clear path forward. Discomfort without direction is just anxiety. I'm not in the anxiety business.
