# Proof

## The Frank Story
Our head of quality control. 28 years with us.

When he walked out, half our institutional knowledge about tolerances, edge cases, and supplier quirks walked out with him. Not the documented stuff — the stuff that was never written down because Frank just knew. The batch from Supplier B that always ran .002 over. The visual tell on the press that preceded a jam. The edge case that happened twice a year and required a judgment call nobody else had ever made.

We spent six months relearning what Frank knew by instinct.

That's the story I tell every prospect. It's not a cautionary tale about someone else. It happened to us. We were the client before we were the consultant.

**Why this works as proof:** It's specific (Frank, 28 years, six months). It's personal (we lived it). And it's the exact story every mid-market manufacturer has a version of — they either already lived it or they can see it coming.

## Credibility Foundation
- 32 years in manufacturing
- Started on the factory floor — not theory, not MBA, factory
- Ran a $4M operation
- Have seen every failure mode — this is the claim, and it holds because the problems we solve are ones engineers couldn't

## Market Position Proof
Competitors charge $250/hour. We charge $180/hour. We have deeper pattern recognition and a proprietary framework. The pricing gap is not a value gap — it's a positioning gap we haven't corrected yet.

This is honest proof: we know we're underpriced relative to what we deliver.

## The Scalability Mirror
We have the same problem our clients have. Our institutional knowledge — the pattern recognition, the failure mode library, the decision logic — is trapped in our heads. We haven't captured it either. This isn't a weakness to hide. It's proof that the problem is universal and hard. If a 32-year veteran with eyes wide open still hasn't solved it for himself, the client shouldn't feel embarrassed that they haven't solved it either.

- "Before and after" knowledge capture examples: what was in the SOP vs. what we found in Phase 1
- Failure modes caught during Capture phase that would have broken the automation project
