# Offer

## Transformation
90 days after working with me, the CEO takes a 2-week vacation and nothing breaks. Their leadership team is making 80% of operational decisions without escalation. Monday morning meetings go from 2 hours of status updates to 45 minutes of strategic decisions. The CEO's calendar has white space for the first time in years. Revenue doesn't dip — it usually grows 10-15% because the team is moving faster without waiting for one person to approve everything.

## Quick Win
Week 2: I present the Decision Audit results. The CEO sees, in black and white, that they personally made 47 decisions last week that should have been made by their directors. The look on their face is always the same — they had no idea. That single document changes the conversation from "I should delegate more" to "I have a structural problem and here's exactly where it is."

## Delivery Mechanics
- **Week 1:** I sit in every recurring meeting. I don't talk. I map the decision flow, the unspoken hierarchy, the bottlenecks. I interview each direct report privately — 30 minutes, one question: "What decision have you wanted to make but felt you couldn't?"
- **Week 2:** Decision Audit presentation. We redesign the decision rights framework together. Who owns what. What needs CEO input vs. CEO approval vs. no CEO involvement at all.
- **Week 3-4:** I attend meetings again but this time I intervene in real-time. When the CEO reaches for a decision that isn't theirs, I flag it on the spot. When a director defers unnecessarily, I redirect.
- **Month 2-3:** Spacing out to weekly check-ins. The new operating rhythm is taking hold. We track the decision distribution weekly.

## DIY Gap
You can read about delegation in a book. You cannot see your own blind spots in a meeting. You need someone who has sat in 200 leadership team meetings and can spot the pattern in the first 30 minutes. My clients are brilliant operators — that's how they built $20M companies. But you can't coach yourself out of habits you can't see. The book tells you what to do. I show you what you're actually doing.
