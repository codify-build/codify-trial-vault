# Offer

## Current Pricing
- Consulting: $180/hour
- Implementation projects: $15K–$40K

## Pricing Reality
Competitors charge $250/hour and deliver less. We've never repositioned. That's a pricing problem, not a value problem.

At $180/hour we are underpriced by at least 28% against the market — and we have stronger proof, deeper pattern recognition, and a proprietary framework they don't. The rate needs to move.

## Transformation
Before: critical knowledge trapped in senior employees' heads, no backup, one retirement away from a six-month setback.

After: decision logic captured, codified, validated, and built into the system — survives the retirement. The operation can train new people against what the best people actually knew, not just what the manuals say.

## Core Mechanism
**Decision Logic Capture.** We map the "if this, then that" rules embedded in your senior people's judgment before any automation work begins. That logic gets codified and validated with the knowledge-holders while they're still here, then becomes the foundation the system is built on.

This is what separates us from every other automation firm: we solve the problem underneath the automation problem.

## Delivery Phases
- **Phase 1 — Capture:** Floor walkthroughs, structured interviews with knowledge-holders, edge-case mapping. We find what's not in the SOPs.
- **Phase 2 — Codify:** Decision logic documented, validated, and translated into system requirements. The veteran signs off on it.
- **Phase 3 — Build:** Automation implementation built on top of the captured logic, not around it.
- **Phase 4 — Transfer:** New operators trained against the captured knowledge, not just the hardware.

## Quick Win
Early in Phase 1, clients typically surface 3–5 failure modes or edge cases they didn't know they were dependent on one person to catch. That alone changes the conversation from "we need to automate" to "we need to capture before we automate."

## DIY Gap
You cannot interview yourself out of blind spots. The knowledge-holder doesn't know what they know that others don't — it's invisible to them because it's instinct. You need someone who has seen enough failure modes to ask the right questions. The SOPs were written by the person who knows the answer. We find the questions they forgot to ask.

## Reposition Target
Stop selling automation. Start selling continuity. The system is the byproduct. Knowledge preservation is the product.
