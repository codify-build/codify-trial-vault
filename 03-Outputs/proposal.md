## The Engagement: Decision Logic Capture

### Phase 1 — Capture (Weeks 1–3)
We identify what's not in the SOPs.

- Floor walkthroughs with knowledge-holders — structured to surface instinctive decisions, not just documented process
- Edge case mapping: what do they check that nobody else would think to check? When do they override the system — and why?
- Supplier and materials knowledge: the quirks, the batches, the relationships that exist in one person's head
- Failure mode inventory: what they watch for, what they know to catch before it becomes a problem

Deliverable: **Knowledge Gap Report** — a frank accounting of what exists in documentation vs. what exists in people.

### Phase 2 — Codify (Weeks 3–5)
We turn instinct into structure.

- Decision logic documented in "if this, then that" format — specific enough that a capable employee new to the role can execute correctly
- Validated by the knowledge-holder: they review, correct, and sign off on what we've captured
- Translated into system requirements where automation is planned — the logic comes first, the system is built around it

Deliverable: **Decision Logic Library** — validated, signed off by the knowledge-holder, ready for system integration or standalone training use.

### Phase 3 — Build (Weeks 5–12, if applicable)
If the engagement includes automation implementation:

- System designed and built on top of the captured logic — not around it
- Edge cases and judgment calls built into the system's decision architecture
- Testing protocol includes edge-case scenarios drawn directly from Phase 1 findings

Deliverable: **Implemented system** with documented logic embedded and traceable.

### Phase 4 — Transfer (Final 2 weeks)
New operators trained against the captured knowledge.

- Training built from the Decision Logic Library, not just the hardware manual
- Knowledge-holder reviews and validates training materials before they walk out the door
- Transfer is complete when a new operator can handle Phase 1's identified edge cases correctly

Deliverable: **Transfer-complete documentation** and trained operator sign-off.
