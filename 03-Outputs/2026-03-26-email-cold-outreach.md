# Cold Outreach Sequence — 3 Emails

## Email 1 — The Insight

**Subject:** your Monday meeting

Marcus here. I work with CEOs who built $10M-50M companies and then got stuck inside them.

Here's what I see in every one of those companies: the CEO is making decisions that should belong to their directors. Not some of them — typically 40% or more. And the team isn't underperforming. They're waiting for permission.

You didn't hire adults to treat them like interns.

I have a 14-day diagnostic called the Decision Audit. I sit in your meetings, talk to your team, and map exactly where your judgment is being used on problems that shouldn't reach you. No surveys. No personality tests.

If what I find doesn't surprise you, you owe me nothing.

— Marcus

---

## Email 2 — The Proof

**Subject:** 47 decisions

A CEO came to me running a $22M company. Smart guy. 65-hour weeks. Leadership team of four who wouldn't make a move without his approval.

He'd read the books. Hired a big-firm consultant — $80K for a strategy deck with nice charts. Nothing changed. Tried to "just delegate more." Lasted two weeks.

In week one I sat in his meetings and didn't say a word. In week two I showed him that he'd personally made 47 decisions the prior week that should have belonged to his directors.

He had no idea.

90 days later his Monday meetings went from 2 hours of status updates to 45 minutes of strategic decisions. He took a two-week vacation. Nothing broke.

Decision debt compounds faster than financial debt. If you're curious what yours looks like, I'll show you in 14 days.

— Marcus

---

## Email 3 — The Ask

**Subject:** one question

If you stepped away from your business for two weeks tomorrow, would it run without you?

If the honest answer is no — or "probably, but I'd be checking my phone the whole time" — that's a structural problem, not a people problem.

I fix that. 90 days. If you don't see a measurable shift in your decision load, I refund the full engagement.

15 minutes on the phone and I'll tell you whether I can help. If I can't, I'll tell you that too.

— Marcus
