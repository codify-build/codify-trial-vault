# LinkedIn Post — Decision Bottlenecks

---

Last year I walked into a $22M manufacturing company. CEO was personally approving every quote over $50K.

That meant he was the bottleneck for 60% of their revenue.

They'd lost a $3M contract because their quote took 11 days. Competitor did it in 3.

He didn't have a staffing problem. He had an architecture problem.

Here's what I've learned after 30 years on shop floors:

Most mid-market manufacturers are running $15-40M businesses on systems they built when they were a $3M shop. The org chart grew. The decision structure didn't.

So the CEO ends up in every meeting. Approving things someone two levels down should own. And saying the same thing I hear every time:

"I built this thing and now it can't run without me."

The fix isn't hiring another VP. It's mapping where decisions actually get made — and how long they take to reach the person who executes them.

Most companies have 6-8 decision layers. Best-in-class have 3.

The gap between those numbers is where the money is hiding.

That CEO? 90 days later, his quoting cycle was down to 2.5 days. He took his first vacation in 4 years. Revenue didn't dip.

The answers are always on the floor, never in the boardroom.
